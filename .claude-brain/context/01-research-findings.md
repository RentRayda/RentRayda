## CORE INSIGHTS

### The kakilala dynamic
- T3 found place in 1 day because she had network (local, compound, councilor's child)
- T1 took 3 months and almost got scammed twice because she had no network
- The informal rental market WORKS for insiders and EXCLUDES outsiders
- No app has replaced kakilala; apps need to MANUFACTURE kakilala-equivalent trust

### The landlord reality
- 0/6 landlords enthusiastic about any platform
- 4/6 fill units in hours or days (L3, L4 Pasig, L5, L6 — no vacancy problem)
- 6/6 want zero fees
- 2/6 want tenant screening tooling explicitly (L4, L5 — both informal, cash, have had problems). L6 does extensive manual screening but does NOT request a tool.
- Only L4's absentee son (Makati) has real vacancy pain

### The tenant reality
- T1: migrant, no network, 3 months, 10 viewings, almost scammed twice
- T2: sophisticated, has own verification system, said NO to platform
- T3: local with community, 1 day, zero pain
- T4: walking distance, fee-averse, said "Airbnb for long-term stays"

### The scam pattern
Same across T1 and T2:
1. Listing on platform (Facebook, Rentpad, Lamudi)
2. Landlord moves conversation to WhatsApp or private message
3. Demands deposit payment BEFORE viewing
4. If paid, disappears
5. If refused, becomes aggressive

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR THE PRODUCT

**Target the narrow sub-segment with real pain:**
- Provincial migrants (from any province to Metro Manila)
- Zero local network (no kakilala)
- Deadline-driven (training start, class start, new job, OFW return)
- Budget ₱5-8K/month
- Most findable version: female BPO new hires at Concentrix/Accenture/Teleperformance (marketing focus, highest concentration, sharpest empathy signal per our 4 female interviews). Other findable versions: students at DLSU/UP/PUP, fresh grads at hospitals/banks, OFW families relocating.
- Platform serves all genders/employer types identically; marketing leans female BPO for story-telling concentration.

**Revenue model must respect:**
- Zero landlord fees (universal finding)
- Transaction-based, not subscription (fees kill adoption)
- Price point around ₱999 (2% of ₱15K deposit = acceptable insurance premium)

**Features must include:**
- Verified listings (scams are real — VALIDATED)
- Verified tenants (L4, L5 requested — EMERGING)
- Deposit escrow (addresses the scam pattern directly)
- Communication facilitation (T1's #1 stated pain)
- Gendered safety signals (female tenant primary target)

**Features must NOT include:**
- Landlord paid features (0/6 want them)
- Search as core value (discovery isn't the problem)
- Chat-first UX (users don't want it)
- Subscriptions (fees kill adoption)
