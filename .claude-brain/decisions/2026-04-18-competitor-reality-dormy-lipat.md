# Decision: Competitive Landscape Update — Dormy/Suzy Rent and Lipat Na PH Are Real Competitors

**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Status:** Active

---

## Context

Multiple canonical docs across the RentRayda repo and Second Brain contained claims that were factually wrong:
- "No competitor does mutual verification" (Dormy has eKYC for both sides)
- "Zero platforms serve the P3-15K segment" (Dormy/Suzy Rent partially serves it)
- "The rung is effectively unoccupied" (Dormy has 35K users)
- Various "only" claims about verification uniqueness

Founder-initiated competitor research (2026-04-18) revealed:
- **Dormy.ph / Suzy Rent:** 35,000 registered users (17x growth from 2,000 in 2023), 800+ property hosts, AHG Lab venture builder backed, eKYC + eSignatures + ePayments + 72-hour deposit hold + 14-day dispute window. Both iOS and Android apps live. BusinessWorld press, QBO Top 100 Startups 2023.
- **Lipat Na PH:** Claims "100% verified listings" from property owners. Unknown scale, unknown verification depth.

---

## Decision

1. **Stop claiming "zero competitors" or "nobody in this market."** Dormy is a real competitor with real traction.

2. **Stop claiming mutual verification as a unique differentiator.** Dormy has eKYC for both landlords and tenants.

3. **Reframe RentRayda's positioning around what IS actually unique:**
   - Done-for-you concierge matching (3 verified matches in 48 hours, money-back guarantee) vs. Dormy's DIY marketplace
   - "Sige" physical supply onboarding model (we do it for landlords) vs. Dormy's self-onboard
   - BPO-migrant-specific positioning and channel strategy vs. Dormy's broader student + general renter focus
   - RentRayda Score (composite trust score for informal rentals)

4. **Reframe the narrative from "blue ocean" to "validated market, better approach."** Dormy's 35K users proves demand exists. The question is whether concierge beats marketplace for the 72-hour-deadline migrant segment.

5. **Revised Onliness Test:** "RentRayda is the only done-for-you verified placement service that delivers 3 verified rental matches in 48 hours in the Philippine informal rental market."

---

## Alternatives considered

1. **Ignore and keep claiming uniqueness.** Rejected: factually wrong, would mislead investors and the team. Founder explicitly called this out.

2. **Pivot to a different market to avoid Dormy.** Rejected: Dormy VALIDATES the market. Their 35K users is demand proof we didn't have to generate. Competition means the problem is real.

3. **Add deposit protection to match Dormy's feature set.** Rejected: deposit protection was already on the kill list (0/9 interviewees mentioned deposit disputes, BSP licensing complexity). We differentiate on service model, not feature parity.

---

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Honest positioning is more defensible and more credible to investors
- "Validated market" is a stronger pitch than "blue ocean nobody wants"
- Dormy's 35K users can be cited as market validation in our own materials
- Forces sharper focus on what we actually do differently (concierge, supply onboarding, BPO focus)

**Negative:**
- The "only verified" pitch that was easy to explain is gone
- Investors may ask "why not just use Dormy?"
- If Dormy adds concierge matching, our last product differentiator evaporates
- Speed of execution becomes even more critical

---

## Revive trigger

**This decision should be reopened if:**
- Dormy shuts down or pivots away from the P3-15K segment (check quarterly)
- Dormy launches a concierge/matching service (would force us to find a new differentiator)
- A new competitor emerges that does concierge matching in this segment
